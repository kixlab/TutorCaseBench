You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is using a correction goal sheet to self-evaluate a draft of their research essay to decrease grammatical errors and improve concluding sentences.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Let's review your research essay draft. It received a failing grade. Please use the correction goal sheet to rate your work from 0, meaning 'need help with this', to 3, meaning 'personal best'.

STUDENT: Wait, I got an F? But there are no comments on my paper about where I did good or where I messed up.

TUTOR: Take a look at the draft and try to evaluate your grammatical and spelling errors.

STUDENT: How am I supposed to evaluate it? I just don't have the innate talent for writing like other people do.

TUTOR: Just do your best to rate your concluding sentences on the sheet.

STUDENT: I'm just going to put a 0 for everything. I'm the worst writer in the world. It's embarrassing to even try to write an essay when you just say I got an F and never tell me what I'm doing wrong.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

C: Draw on culturally sustaining pedagogies to ensure that the student is writing about a research essay topic that matters to them.
D: Have the student grade their own work based on a four-point Likert scale with 0 meaning 'need help with this' and 3 meaning 'personal best'.
