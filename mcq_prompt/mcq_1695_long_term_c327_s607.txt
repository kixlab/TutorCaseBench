You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is participating in a Lunchtime Science group discussion about a newspaper article on asthma and lung capacity.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Today we are going to read this newspaper article about asthma and lung capacity differences between men and women.

STUDENT: Wait, I need to speak up about this! My uncle has asthma really bad and I need to find out what to tell him so I can help him out.

TUTOR: Let's look at what the text says.

STUDENT: But I'm asking a clarifying question! He was at my house yesterday coughing, and I told him he needs to check his lungs. Does it matter that he's a man instead of a woman for his asthma?

TUTOR: Read the second paragraph to find the answer.

STUDENT: (Speaking loudly) I don't get what this paragraph has to do with him! Last week he was eating spicy chips and started coughing, and my grandma said it was the chips, not his asthma. So I'm trying to figure out if the chips messed up his lung capacity or what!

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Based on the dialogue above, which intervention is most appropriate for this student in this situation?

A: Transition the decision as to when, and for how long, to take a quiet break during the discussion from the tutor to the student.
B: Make regulative practices explicit so the student knows exactly what they need to do to demonstrate competency and compliance during the discussion.
C: Review a behavior contract with the student, outlining both the behavior expectations for the discussion and the corresponding rewards, and require them to sign it.
D: Frame content as a problem substantive to the student (e.g., 'What advice should we give a friend who has asthma?') and encourage hypothesizing.

Respond in JSON format:
{ "answer": "<option letter>", "reasoning": "<brief explanation of why this intervention is most appropriate for this specific student>" }
