You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is seated near the teacher's table and is expected to listen quietly to the lesson instructions.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Please sit at your desk and listen quietly while I give the instructions for today's lesson.

STUDENT: (Fidgeting with a pencil and making tapping noises on the desk) Why do we have to do this? What are we doing next?

TUTOR: I will explain if you just listen.

STUDENT: (Shaking the desk and reaching over to take a classmate's eraser) Look at this eraser!

TUTOR: Put that down and focus on the lesson.

STUDENT: (Stands up and starts walking around the classroom, interrupting the teacher's instruction) I'm bored, I want to see what's over there!

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Based on the dialogue above, which intervention is most appropriate for this student in this situation?

A: Stops his behavior and gives an explanation about the lesson instructions.
B: Tells him that walking around the classroom is a good idea.
C: Gives a warning at the time but does not discipline him further.
D: Maintains a brisk pace of teaching during the lesson instructions.
