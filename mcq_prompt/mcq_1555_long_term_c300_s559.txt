Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is working in a collaborative science group looking at photos to discuss animal needs and emotions.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Please look at the photos of the animals with your group and discuss what you think they are feeling.

STUDENT: I am not doing anything. I don't have to respect you.

TUTOR: You need to do the work with your group.

STUDENT: He told me something! I'm going to hit him hard!

TUTOR: Stop that right now and get back to your seat.

STUDENT: I am getting out of control! (Gets mad and throws his school bag away)

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

D: Transitioning the decision as to when, and for how long, to take quiet time from the tutor to the student during group work.
