You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is sitting at a table with peers during writing workshop, expected to create a written product for the 'What my name means to me' activity.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Please start working on your written product for the 'What my name means to me' activity.

STUDENT: I dunno what to write. I’m not a good writer, you know?

TUTOR: You need to concentrate and focus on your work.

STUDENT: (Keeps talking to peers, exposing thoughts verbally but not writing) I have a lot of names. People call me Rafael, Rafa, Rafi, Rafito, Muniz … These are all my names.

TUTOR: Please stop talking and write your sentences down on your paper.

STUDENT: (Looks blankly for several seconds, then runs around the table reaching out to peers and almost touching them) Got you! You’re it! You can’t get me!

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Based on the dialogue above, which intervention is most appropriate for this student in this situation?

B: Provide worksheets with dotted letters on them for the student to trace during the writing workshop, focusing strictly on the physical mechanics of the written product.
C: Conduct careful pre-intervention functional assessments to identify specific relationships between the writing workshop environment and the student's behavior before proceeding with the activity.
D: Learn about the student's individual preferences, interests, and processes in a cultural anthropological way rather than immediately rating their practices against the master narrative of a good student.
