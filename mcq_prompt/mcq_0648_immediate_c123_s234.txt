You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is using drawing materials to draw a picture in their journal about the upcoming move to the new school.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Please use your journals and drawing materials to draw a picture of what the new school will look like or how you are feeling about the move.

STUDENT: (Draws a picture of the outside area) I will miss the playground because I would play here.

TUTOR: I see. What else are you adding to your journal?

STUDENT: (Points to a different part of the page) And this is where we did this project.

TUTOR: Can you describe your picture to me?

STUDENT: (Looks sad and nervous) I don't want to move. I like this school.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Based on the dialogue above, which intervention is most appropriate for this student in this situation?

A: Take the student to another room to calm them down.
B: Shift the student's focus to a progressive and methodical body scan.
C: Ask the student to describe their picture in the journal.
D: Deliberately ignore the negative behavior, including motor and verbal actions.
