followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is sitting at class tables with photos laid out to suggest dialogue and ideas about a story plot with peers.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Let's look at the photos laid out on the tables and suggest some dialogue and ideas about the plot for our story.

TUTOR: Let's listen to your classmate's suggestion for the story first.

STUDENT: No, I'm not listening to them! My plot idea is better!

TUTOR: What do you think about what your peer just said?

STUDENT: (Interrupting) That's a bad idea! I'm not waiting until you ask me to respond, I want to talk about my ideas now!

Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

D: Respond by enthusiastically praising the student for coming up with another idea so quickly and encouraging them to keep going.
