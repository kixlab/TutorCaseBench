SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is in a laboratory setting following prescriptive guidelines to examine the chemical components of cigarette smoke.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Please look at your prescriptive guidelines for the cigarette smoke lab. Follow the steps exactly as they are written to examine the chemical components.

STUDENT: I'm looking at these steps, but this module is not easy to understand. It just gives us a list to copy.

TUTOR: Just read the next step on the sheet and do what it says.

STUDENT: But doing it this way doesn't make us think. There is no room for us to plan or to decide how to set up the equipment.

TUTOR: Try to keep working through the list.

STUDENT: (Pushes the guideline sheet away) I'm not doing this. It's too much prescriptive and rigid, and it doesn't let us participate or figure out how to deal with unexpected difficulties.

Question
