You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is sitting with the teaching assistant to complete a written exam.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Here is the next question on your written exam. Please read it and write your answer.

STUDENT: (Avoids social eye contact and looks away from the exam paper.)

TUTOR: What do you think the answer is? Keep trying.

STUDENT: I want that the YouTube channel where I listen to music is always on.

TUTOR: We need to finish the written exam first.

STUDENT: (Loses attention completely, stands up due to a need for constant movement, and starts walking toward the door to go out to the school yard without prior notice.)

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

C: Offers listening to music on YouTube as a reward for completing the rest of the written exam.
D: Continues with the written exam rather than interrupting the flow of the task to address the student's behavior.
