You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is participating in a 30-minute whole-class reading of the picture book 'What Do You Do with an Idea?' and is asked to discuss the story.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: The teacher has been reading 'What Do You Do with an Idea?' to the class for 30 minutes. Do you know what is happening in the story?

STUDENT: (Makes a weak smile and nods quietly.)

TUTOR: That's good. What did the boy do with his idea?

STUDENT: (Sits quietly and says nothing, with a depressed and helpless look on her face.)

TUTOR: You can tell me. What do you think?

STUDENT: Umm…honestly…I have no idea what she’s talking about. I have been sitting quietly just like this. (Watches her classmates and gets up from her seat to move to the same area of the classroom when they do.)

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Based on the dialogue above, which intervention is most appropriate for this student in this situation?

A: Allow the student to select a positive reinforcer to work for before beginning the reading activity, and then ensure they earn that reinforcer when they finish the discussion, or at least make an honest attempt.

C: Avoid using assigned whole-class picture books and instead allow the student to self-select books that are appropriate for their reading levels and that interest them during the 30-minute reading block.
D: Instruct the student to take out a sheet of paper, write the title of the picture book, and write as much as they can recall from beginning to end, reminding them it is a retelling, not a summary.
