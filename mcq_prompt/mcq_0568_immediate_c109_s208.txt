followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student approaches the lecturer's desk at the end of the lesson to hand in a physical copy of a manifesto she posted on the course's internet forum.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Class is over now. Did you need to hand something in?

STUDENT: (Approaches the desk and hands over a copy of a letter) I could not find your box and did not have your address.

TUTOR: Thank you for handing this to me. What is this letter regarding?

STUDENT: It is the manifesto I published on the course's internet forum. Today’s lesson was no different from previous lessons. I want to protest against the level, the conduct of the teacher and the way the material is taught.

TUTOR: I see. What specific concerns are you raising in this document?

STUDENT: There are discussions of observations, which may have been conducted without obtaining consent. The course is conducted in a theatrical and joking manner, what I would call worthless psychology. I consider this to be inappropriate for a religious university and unsuitable for the students.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

C: Remain a silent observer and passive participant during the exchange, making no comments on the student's manifesto or her interactions regarding the course.
D: Provide direct reassurance, validate the difficulty of the course material, and highlight the student's hard work and successful performance during the recent lessons.
