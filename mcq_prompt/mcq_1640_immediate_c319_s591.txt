followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Gabriel is sitting in his general education classroom and is asked to begin working on a modified academic assignment.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Gabriel, here is your modified assignment for today. Please get started.

STUDENT: I like to come to my school, Apple Elementary. I like the adults at my school, like Mrs. Jones, Mr. Bell, and Mrs. Scott.

TUTOR: That is nice. Now please look at your paper and begin.

STUDENT: There are lots of things I like at school. (Does not look at the assignment)

TUTOR: You need to participate and do your work right now.

STUDENT: (Pushes the paper away and begins to have an emotional outburst) No! I am refusing to participate in this assignment!

Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────
