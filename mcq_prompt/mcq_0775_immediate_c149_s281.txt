──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Stephanye is participating in a whole-class read-aloud discussion of the book 'Separate is Never Equal' about skin color and segregation.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: We are reading 'Separate is Never Equal' and talking about skin color. Kristoff just said that you have brown skin, Stephanye. Do you want to respond to that?

STUDENT: I want to say that Kristoff said that I am Brown, and it looks like I am Brown, but I am actually Black.

TUTOR: I see. What else would you like to share with the class?

STUDENT: Um (pauses), my mom and her mom got treated differently because they were Black.

TUTOR: How do you know that happened to them?

STUDENT: They just (pauses). I’m not sure. I just know it. We know that in my family.
