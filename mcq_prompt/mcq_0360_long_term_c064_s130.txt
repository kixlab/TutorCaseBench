Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is sitting in a circle with the group, waiting for a turn to speak about the stimulus text.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: We have finished reading the stimulus text. Please raise your hand if you want to share your thoughts with the circle, and wait until I call on you.

STUDENT: (Raises hand and starts waving it in the face of the student sitting next to him) Pick me, I want to talk right now!

TUTOR: We need to wait for everyone to be ready. Just hold on.

STUDENT: Sometimes it’s really diﬃcult, you need to follow the story and you need to think about what the person’s saying and you need to agree or disagree and you know someone’s not going to get upset.

TUTOR: Try your best to wait your turn.

STUDENT: (Pushes his chair back loudly and starts shouting over the other students) I can't remember what I was going to say now! I'm not waiting anymore!

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

D: Require that students cannot reference an outside authority for their agreement or disagreement, prompting them to think and reason for themselves during the discussion.
