SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is participating in a small group pull-out session and is asked to write a personal memory goal on their goal card.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Let's take out our goal cards. Today we are learning about Strengthening Your Memory, so please write down a goal to remember one fact learned in class tomorrow.

STUDENT: I don't really need to write a goal for that. I'm already really competent at remembering things. I'd give myself a 90% on that already.

TUTOR: What do you think you could write down? Just try to put one goal on your card.

STUDENT: (Pushes the goal card away) I have a lot of class assignments and homework I haven't finished yet. I should be doing that.

TUTOR: We need to finish this activity for our intervention block. Keep trying.

STUDENT: I'm not enjoying being in this small group. Everyone else in homeroom is using this time to work on their homework. I don't like that I 'missed intervention' in the classroom. If you ask me what I like about being pulled out here, I'd say 'Nothing except leaving.'
