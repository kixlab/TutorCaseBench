──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is reviewing a peer's contribution during a mixed-ability group work session in an introductory education course.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Please review the section your group member just completed for the project.

STUDENT: I always appreciate contribution of all members. However, I have to say I feel safer if the contribution comes from a good student. Whenever we receive products from a less able member, we need to spend time rechecking so it takes our time.

TUTOR: What are you going to do with their section?

STUDENT: I am turning to do my own work. I do not trust their contributions and I am being held back by slower teammates.

TUTOR: Have you tried talking to them when they express their ideas?

STUDENT: When they get stuck, I just 'jump into their mouth'. I go faster and I do not clearly explain information to the lower-achieving students.
