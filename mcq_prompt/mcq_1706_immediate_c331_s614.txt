followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is expected to listen to the teacher and answer questions during a general classroom lesson while seated near peers.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: We are going to go over the lesson now. Please sit at your desk, listen carefully, and be ready to answer questions.

STUDENT: (Looks outside through the window and starts playing with a pencil instead of listening.)

TUTOR: Please pay attention to the lesson.

STUDENT: (Turns around to the peer sitting at the desk behind them) Look at what I can do with my pencil.

TUTOR: Stop talking to your friends. You need to sit quietly and listen to me right now.

STUDENT: (Stands up without permission, walks over to the friend's desk, and grabs their eraser while talking aloud) I am taking this!

Question
