You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The class is sitting on the carpet after lunch listening to a read-aloud of 'Ereth's Birthday'.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Alright everyone, let's continue reading 'Ereth's Birthday' on the carpet. Ereth just said his usual grumpy phrase. What do we think about what just happened?

STUDENT: (Repeats the phrase from the book, guffaws, and looks around at the other students) Um, unnecessary?

TUTOR: Let's keep reading to see what happens to him next.

STUDENT: (Fidgets, faces away from the teacher, and makes a funny face to get another student's attention) He didn’t get killed. ... You mean the cage trap.

TUTOR: Please remember to raise your hand if you want to share your thoughts.

STUDENT: (Laughs loudly and interrupts again) No! ... The weak don’t always have to help the strong because if they’re a criminal or something, that might make it worse.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Based on the dialogue above, which intervention is most appropriate for this student in this situation?

C: Play 'devil's advocate' or present an opposing argument about the book's characters to challenge the student's thinking during the discussion.
D: Invite the students to engage as real readers and participate in authentic conversations with others about what they read in the book.
