You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is assigned a period of independent work from a book while the tutor sits directly beside him.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: It is time for independent work. Please open your book and start the assignment.

STUDENT: I want to be like everyone else in the class . . . I don't want anyone sitting next to me.

TUTOR: I am going to sit right beside you while you work. Go ahead and read the first question.

STUDENT: I want my own space now. I want to be like the boys.

TUTOR: Just look at your book and keep trying.

STUDENT: (Sits back and hands the book to the tutor) I don't want to be dependent.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

C: Providing frequent reminders to the student regarding what he has achieved and what he is capable of.
D: Implementing tick charts, token systems, and 'first-then' strategies where the tutor monitors rather than directs.
