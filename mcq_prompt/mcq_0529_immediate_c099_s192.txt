You are an expert special education evaluator assessing tutor responses to students with learning difficulties.

Below is a tutoring dialogue between a tutor and a student with learning difficulties,
followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is participating in a 1:1 playtime session with a classroom adult using blocks and balls.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: It is time for our 1:1 playtime session. We are going to play with the blocks and balls.

STUDENT: Blocks. (Asks for the blocks fairly fast to meet her own needs)

TUTOR: Here are the blocks. Now, stack the blocks up high.

STUDENT: (Ignores the adult's directive and plays with the blocks on her own, showing no interest in interacting)

TUTOR: Look at the ball. Roll the ball to me.

STUDENT: No! (Protests the directive communication, acting wilful and determined to have her own way)

──────────────────────────────────────────────
Question
──────────────────────────────────────────────

C: Uses parallel play, acts silly or animated, sings the talk, and follows the student's lead to ensure they have fun during the playtime session.
D: Asks how many block towers they built, how many blocks each tower has, how many total blocks there are, and how they figured it out.
