followed by a multiple-choice question about the most appropriate intervention.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is participating in a one-on-one interview to discuss the Heinz Dilemma and make an issue choice.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Let's look at the Heinz Dilemma. Should Heinz steal the drug?

STUDENT: No. It's against the law and he will get punished.

TUTOR: Would Heinz's love for his wife justify his stealing the drug to save her life?

STUDENT: (Softly) Yeah.

TUTOR: Okay, so your choice is yes, he should steal the drug to save her?

STUDENT: (Begins shaking her head no as the tutor speaks)

Question
