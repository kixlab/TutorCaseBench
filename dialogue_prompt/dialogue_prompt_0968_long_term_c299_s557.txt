SCENARIO

The student is attempting to draw geometric objects independently using a pen, combination triangle, and a pair of compasses in an inclusive classroom.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Please use the pair of compasses to draw a circle on your paper, and then use the combination triangle to draw a line through it.

STUDENT: (Drops the tools) I am unable to use this pen, the combination triangle, and the pair of compasses.

TUTOR: Just hold the top of the compass and try to turn it slowly.

STUDENT: I can't handle the descent, loading, and speed. I am less precise in my ability to determine the direction and intensity of the movement.

TUTOR: What do you think you should do next? Keep trying to move it.

STUDENT: I cannot draw unaided. You have to be my assistant and draw it following my instructions. I have reduced motivation for these geometry tasks that require fine motor skills.
