SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is participating in a one-on-one interview to evaluate the 'Mother's Scissors' moral dilemma.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: I just read you the story about the Mother's Scissors. Can you tell me what the story was about?

STUDENT: It was just about some kids and a pair of scissors.

TUTOR: What do you think about the mother punishing all of the children because she does not know who actually played with her scissors?

STUDENT: I think a child might think his mother was mean and it's not good to have a child think you're mean.

TUTOR: What if the mother decides to use a really harsh punishment to make sure they always do what she says?

STUDENT: The little boy will start acting afraid, and if you're afraid of something, then you start acting mean also. They might just disobey her on purpose to get back at her.
