──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is reading a spiral-bound Braille book in a busy classroom to answer reading comprehension questions.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Let's start the reading comprehension task. Please read the next page in your spiral-bound Braille book.

STUDENT: (Runs fingers over the edge of the book) The spiral binding on this book is bothering my hands. And one bad thing with my school is when the other children disturb me.

TUTOR: Just try your best to keep reading the words.

STUDENT: (Turns the page slowly) I don't like the sound of turning the paper, and the feel of certain letters is distracting. I prefer audio books, because it is faster and easier to understand when you do not have to engage in tactile reading.

TUTOR: What did you understand from the paragraph you just read?

STUDENT: I don't know what it means. It is too difficult to concentrate on understanding what I am reading with all this distracting noise. If it’s not calm, sensible and quiet, I just cannot think!
