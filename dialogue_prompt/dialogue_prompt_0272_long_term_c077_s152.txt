DIALOGUE

TUTOR: Today we are going to try a new activity to practice using your Braille skills in real life. I have some new folders here, and I want you to read the labels and sort them quickly.

STUDENT: I am happy doing what I am used to do. It’s difficult when you have to think fast and answer fast and do things fast all the time.

TUTOR: It is important to understand how you can use skills you learn in school in other contexts. Just give it a try and read the first label.

STUDENT: (Touches the Braille label) It says 'Math'. No, wait, I read the dots wrong. I made a mistake.

TUTOR: That's fine, don't worry about it. Just leave it and move on to the next folder.

STUDENT: I am very worried. I want the mistake to be corrected immediately. I can't think about anything else until we fix it.
