SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The student is using a desktop computer with a mouse and keyboard to complete a geometry activity in an inclusive classroom.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Let's start the geometry activity on the computer. Please use the mouse to click and drag the shape to the correct spot on the screen.

STUDENT: (Tries to move the mouse but the cursor shoots across the screen) I can't get it to go where I want. It's moving too fast.

TUTOR: Just try to move it a little slower and click the button.

STUDENT: (Presses the mouse button heavily and misses the shape) I missed it again. I am not an active user of computers, I don't know how to do this.

TUTOR: Keep trying, you just need to practice using the mouse.

STUDENT: (Pushes the mouse away, looking stressed) I can't do this! It's too hard to control the speed and intensity, and I keep messing up the geometry task. I don't want to do this anymore.
