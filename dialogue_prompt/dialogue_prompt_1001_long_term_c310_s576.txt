SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Ofra is sitting down to take her first matriculation exam with newly assigned accommodations available to her.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Here is your first matriculation exam. You have extra time and the option for me to read the questions aloud to you.

STUDENT: Come on, who needs it? I don’t always need the reading aloud.

TUTOR: Okay, you can start the exam now.

STUDENT: I did not understand why I was given these things, and like everything happens so quickly... until you take the time to digest the fact that you have such accommodations and these are the first matriculation exams and everything is new, everything goes quickly.

TUTOR: Try to read the first question and see how it goes.

STUDENT: It inhibits very much when I come to the exam with discomfort and with a special block... nothing was really stable to let me really calm myself. I sometimes felt as if I asked too much... Sorry, but can someone read aloud to me? Sorry, but I need extra time.
