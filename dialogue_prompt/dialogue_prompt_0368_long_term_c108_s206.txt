SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

James is participating in a small group journaling and poetry activity in the classroom.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Alright everyone, for this small group activity, please open your journals and write a short poem about your weekend.

STUDENT: I'm not writing about my weekend. It was just stress at home. I thought I'd just lost an hour just sitting there.

TUTOR: Just try to write down whatever comes to mind on the page, James.

STUDENT: (Glares at a peer across the table) What are you looking at? You think I took your pen? Just because of my brother? Twenty quid doesn't just go randomly missing and you forget about it, is that what you're thinking? Why can't you just be good?

TUTOR: James, please ignore the others and look at your own journal.

STUDENT: (Slams hands on the desk) No! Everyone just expects me to be this angry person! I'm not doing this! (Stands up aggressively towards the peer) I'm just going to go in on the first thought that I've had!
