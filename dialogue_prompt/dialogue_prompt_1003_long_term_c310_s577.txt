──────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENARIO
──────────────────────────────────────────────

Ofra is asked to read an assigned text in the classroom and needs to use her reading aloud accommodations.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
DIALOGUE
──────────────────────────────────────────────

TUTOR: Ofra, please start reading the assigned text for today's lesson so we can go over the questions.

STUDENT: In the beginning, I said: 'Come on, who needs it?' but slowly I saw that I'll take it. I need my accommodations like being read to aloud.

TUTOR: I see. Do you have your tape-recorder with you to use for the reading?

STUDENT: No. I really insisted on this. I knew that this was what I deserved and needed to bypass my difficulties.

TUTOR: If you didn't bring the tape-recorder yourself, you will just have to read the text on your own today.

STUDENT: The student does not always have to run, bring the tape-recorder and say sorry for needing to be read aloud to. No! It is a thing that he deserves. It is the school’s commitment. I have so much anger towards the educational system that they don’t prepare the teachers for this thing!
