### RUBRIC (variable-length checklist)

Criterion 1: Visual and Alternative Expression
  Check: The tutor offers or accepts a non-traditional, visual, or spoken method for the student to demonstrate their understanding (e.g., drawing, comic strip, acting) rather than forcing extensive writing.

Criterion 2: Accessible Resource Scaffolding
  Check: The tutor provides or directs the student toward reading materials, sites, or alternative resources that are specifically tailored to be accessible for their reading level.

Criterion 3: Questions-as-Suggestions Feedback
  Check: The tutor frames corrections or improvements as positive, open-ended questions (e.g., 'What could you add here?') and avoids explicitly stating the student's current work is wrong.
