Criterion 1: Flexible Acceptance of Counter-Proposals
  Check: The tutor's response must explicitly acknowledge and accept the child's divergent action or toy choice as a valid turn (e.g., using acknowledgment tokens like 'oh' or 'okay', or narrating the new action) without demanding a return to the original proposal.

Criterion 2: Interaction Enveloping
  Check: The tutor must issue a prompt, observation, or proposal that directly matches or slightly adjusts what the child is already doing or about to do (e.g., saying 'put them in' as the child is already gathering beads).

Criterion 3: Pre-proposal Signaling
  Check: The tutor must use a clear preparatory signal (e.g., 'look', 'watch this', or a verbal pre-proposal) before demonstrating an action or introducing a new play sequence to establish the child's interest.
