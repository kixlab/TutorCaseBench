### RUBRIC (variable-length checklist)
────────────────────────────────

Criterion 1: Explicit Vocabulary Translation
  Check: The tutor explicitly defines, translates, or emphasizes specific mathematical vocabulary (e.g., comparative terms or division phrases) to ensure correct operational understanding.

Criterion 2: Visual-to-Model Bridging
  Check: The tutor explicitly prompts the student to connect a visual representation (like drawings or arrows) to specific parts of a formal model diagram (e.g., by underlining words or pointing to specific boxes).

Criterion 3: Targeted Quantity Prompting
  Check: The tutor asks connected, specific questions that direct the student's attention to identifying the distinct quantities involved and their specific roles (e.g., 'Which part tells us the value of each group?').

Criterion 4: Explicit Operation Notation
  Check: The tutor instructs the student to explicitly write down the name of the operation (e.g., 'division', 'multiplication') and/or use a simplified notation format like a fraction.
