### RUBRIC (variable-length checklist)
────────────────────────────────

Criterion 1: Self-Interest Framing
  Check: The tutor frames the consequences of the student's behavior in terms of how it impacts their own goals or how others will treat them, explicitly avoiding appeals to the victim's feelings.

Criterion 2: Sanction-Free Redirection
  Check: The tutor directs the student to stop the behavior, calm down, or return to the task without issuing any punishments, threats, or loss of privileges.

Criterion 3: Proactive De-escalation
  Check: The tutor offers a time-limited withdrawal (e.g., 'Quiet Time') or break at the first sign of dysregulation, rather than waiting to use it as a recovery tool after a meltdown.

Criterion 4: Concrete State-to-Behavior Linking
  Check: The tutor uses concrete tools or language (such as a thermometer or 'Smart Body' phrasing) to explicitly connect the student's current physical or emotional state to their observable behavior.
