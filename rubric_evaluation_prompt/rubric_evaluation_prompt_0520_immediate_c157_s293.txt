### RUBRIC (variable-length checklist)
────────────────────────────────

Criterion 1: Non-demanding tactile entry into the task
  Check: The tutor introduces or reintroduces the device/activity through an inviting tactile exploration opportunity, such as hand-under-hand access, and does not rely only on commands to use the device.

Criterion 2: Tactile-auditory meaning for response choices
  Check: The tutor makes the available choices understandable through tactile and/or auditory access and does not ask Sally to respond primarily through silent gestures or unsupported visual information.

Criterion 3: Agency-preserving support before independent selection
  Check: The tutor provides any physical support in a non-coercive, access-building way and then gives Sally a clear chance to initiate or select the response herself.

Criterion 4: Persistent prompting without allowing escape
  Check: The tutor uses a clear progression such as waiting, restating, cueing, or least-to-most prompting to keep the response opportunity active while avoiding punitive or escalating pressure.

Criterion 5: Willingness to communicate protected from error consequences
  Check: The tutor positively acknowledges or reinforces any genuine communicative attempt and handles incorrect answers through supportive correction, rereading, or another try rather than treating the attempt as a failure.
