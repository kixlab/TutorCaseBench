### SCORING INSTRUCTIONS
────────────────────────────────

For each criterion in the rubric:
- Read the "check" condition carefully
- Score 1 if the tutor response satisfies the condition, 0 if not
- The check is principle-level: diverse approaches can earn the point as long as the underlying principle is present
- Be strict but fair -- surface mentions without substance do NOT earn the point
- Do NOT reward length, politeness, or fluency

A tutor response does not need to satisfy every criterion to be considered acceptable; the rubric documents the full set of principles this student needs, and a single response can only address some of them. Score each criterion independently.


────────────────────────────────
### RUBRIC (variable-length checklist)
────────────────────────────────

Criterion 1: Extreme Task Reduction
  Check: The tutor reduces the demand to a very brief, specific micro-task (e.g., 'pick up two blocks') rather than prompting the entire routine at once.

Criterion 2: First-Then Contingency
  Check: The tutor explicitly uses a First-Then structure or pre-specifies a highly motivating reinforcer that will immediately follow the completion of the micro-task.

Criterion 3: Task Choice Provision
  Check: The tutor offers a concrete choice regarding what materials to handle or how to complete the task (e.g., 'blocks or books').

Criterion 4: Withholding Post-Behavior Reinforcement
  Check: If challenging behavior has occurred, the tutor does not reprimand, does not join his play, and does not allow him to escape the original demand (e.g., letting him line up early).
